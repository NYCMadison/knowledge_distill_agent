Harnessing the full potential of hydrogen from green electricity sources
The EU is intending to have all sectors of the economy at net zero greenhouse gas production by 2050. But heavy industry and transport can be challenging to electrify directly. So renewable, and low-carbon hydrogen, and its derivatives, are key tools in the box.

From less than 2 % of our consumption in 2022, by 2050, it is estimated that hydrogen will account for around 10 % of the EU’s energy needs.

To get there, we need to create a stronger demand for the fuel, boost transport and storage networks and secure the renewable affordable electricity needed to generate cost-effective hydrogen.

Our three projects today, all of which received support from the EU’s research and innovation funding, have some solutions.
